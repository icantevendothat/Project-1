Mass incarceration in the United States is an issue that has garnered significant attention in recent years, due to the seemingly outlandish number of people who are sent to prison by a justice system that seems to have no other solution. With the highest incarceration rate in the world, the U.S. prison system has seen a staggering increase in the number of individuals behind bars, disproportionately affecting communities of color. This phenomenon is often attributed to a combination of factors, including mandatory minimum sentencing laws, the war on drugs, and a profit-driven private prison industry. Mass incarceration not only exacts a heavy toll on those within the system but also on society as a whole, as it perpetuates cycles of poverty and disenfranchisement, disrupts families, and strains government resources. Addressing this issue involves comprehensive criminal justice reform efforts aimed at reducing the reliance on incarceration and promoting alternative approaches to rehabilitation and community support.
